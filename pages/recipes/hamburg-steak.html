Japanese style Hamburg Steak

Description

This is a western influenced Japanese dish that is commonly served in Japanese restaurants as well as made at home. Despite it being named with the name steak, this dish is actually made from a combination of minced pork and beef in a specific beef/pork ratio either 2:1 (8 oz/4 oz) or 3:1 (9 oz/3 oz). You can also stuff it with cheese if you so desire. This was a dish I made for my wife that I served with mashed potatoes and roasted vegetables. A demi glace sauce is usually served on top but as real demi glace is difficult to make you can use store bought demi glace mix or sauce. An alternate option would be creating a pan sauce.

Ingredients

  • 300grams of pork and beef minced. (Creates servings of 2 in 150 grams each
  • Dry Panko bread crumbs
  • 1 Whole egg
  • Salt and pepper
  • (If stuffing)A slice or shreds of any cheese of your liking

Instructions

  1. In a bowl combine meat, breadcrumbs, egg, and salt & pepper in a bowl. Combine with hands until all ingredients are well mixed together.
  2. From a ball with the mixture that should be anywhere from 100 to 200 grams depending on how big you would like it. Note: If stuffing your hamburg steak with cheese add the cheese to the inside of your ball by forming the mixture into a ball around it.
  3. Cook your hamburg steak on a pan and brown both sides. Once both sides are browned add a little bit of water to the pan and cover with a lid. The hamburg steaks will be steaming now for about 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the steaks from the pan and let rest for 5 minutes.
  5. Serve with a demi glace sauce or pan sauce. Also add any vegetable sides of your choice.
